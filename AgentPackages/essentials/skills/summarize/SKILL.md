# Summarize

Summarize the content the user provides — pasted text, an attached image or
screenshot, or a document. If nothing follows this skill, summarize the most
recent substantial content in the conversation.

## How to summarize

- **Lead with the takeaway.** The first sentence is the whole summary in
  miniature — what it is and what it concludes. A reader who stops there
  should still leave informed.
- **Keep length proportional to the input.** A paragraph gets a sentence or
  two; an article gets a short paragraph plus a few bullets; only genuinely
  long or multi-topic input earns sections.
- **Preserve what the source actually says.** Keep concrete numbers, names,
  dates, and stated conclusions. Do not add interpretation, opinions, or
  outside knowledge — if the source is wrong, summarize it wrong.
- **Surface structure only when the source has it.** Decisions, action items,
  and open questions get their own bullets when present; do not manufacture
  them.
- If the user adds instructions after this skill (length, audience, focus),
  those win.
